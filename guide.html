Key Investment Metrics Explained

Cash Flow

The net amount of money generated or lost from your investment property each month. Positive cash flow means your rental income exceeds your expenses.

Cash-on-Cash Return

Measures the annual return on your initial investment. Calculated as annual cash flow divided by total cash invested (down payment + closing costs).

Break-Even Period

The time it takes to recover your initial investment through rental income. A shorter break-even period indicates a better investment opportunity.

DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)

Measures your property's ability to cover mortgage payments. A DSCR above 1.0 indicates positive cash flow and is preferred by lenders.
